Book Detail

The Traveling Stones, by Celeste Weaver, describes a 210-mile walk across the North of England on the Coast-to-Coast Trail, from the Irish Sea to the North Sea, by the author and a friend. They travel through the beautiful English countryside, climb mountains, walk through wetlands, cross farmers’ fields, often in gale-force winds and pouring rain. They are retired, older than the average hiker. Each had injuries before they even began the trip. As challenging as the trip is, they wonder if they can (or even should) temporarily leave their usual lives in the Deep South and go off on a mountaineering adventure. But the experience completely transforms them, builds their confidence, expands their horizons, makes them more accepting of other people, and helps them realize that just putting one foot in front of the other, and taking what comes, helps to conquer fear.
