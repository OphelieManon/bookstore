Book Detail

Winter is coming. Such is the stern motto of House Stark, the northernmost of the fiefdoms that owe allegiance to King Robert Baratheon in far-off King’s Landing. There Eddard Stark of Winterfell rules in Robert’s name. There his family dwells in peace and comfort: his proud wife, Catelyn; his sons Robb, Brandon, and Rickon; his daughters Sansa and Arya; and his bastard son, Jon Snow. Far to the north, behind the towering Wall, lie savage Wildings and worse—unnatural things relegated to myth during the centuries-long summer, but proving all too real and all too deadly in the turning of the season.

Review

By Jessica, April 6, 2023

I watched the show years ago and finally decided to start on the novels. I am not the biggest fantasy-genre fan (even though Dune is one of my favorite books), but I loved this book. Yes, there are a lot of details about minor characters, their outfit details could have been left out or conversations shortened, but honestly I feel the book was perfectly paced and those small details show how much the author cared about every individual character. The end of every chapter left me wanting more. Can't wait to start the next book!
