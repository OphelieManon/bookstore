Book Detail

Futaba Yoshioka has encountered her first love again in high school, but he seems different from the boy she once knew. Futaba feels that the distance between her and Kou has grown since he and Yui have started keeping in touch. Then something unexpected happens between Futaba and Kou at the school festival.

Review

By Jasmine, January 20, 2022

This volume isn't bad. The art is wonderful, for example. We also learn more background story on what Kou was up to after Junior High School. Unfortunately, Sakisaka decided to throw up two roadblocks in Kou's and Futaba's relationship, and the organic flow of the story has suffered. I feel that Narumi—Kou's roadblock—works with the story well. She provides an opportunity for us to learn more about Kou. What I dislike is Kou's odd apathy towards Futaba's feelings. His hesitation and conflict feels contrived—as if the real story has been kicked down the road to make room for padding. Kikuchi—Futaba's roadblock—is the worse contrivance of the two. He is preying on Futaba's pain for his own desires. I've read this volume before, and I forgot how much I detest this storyline. Kou's insensitivity to Futaba hurts her because the author needed Futaba to be available for Kikuchi. I'm not looking forward to the next volume.
